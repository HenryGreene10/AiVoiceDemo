Where should archaeologists dig next? The winners of this OpenAI contest can tell them.

Machine learning points to dozens of likely archaeological sites hidden across the Amazon rainforest.

By National Geographic Staff

The Amazon basin conceals traces of ancient cities beneath dense rainforest canopies. Now, competition-winning models trained on remote-sensing data are flagging dozens of promising sites where archaeologists might dig next.

Using LiDAR, DEM rasters, and geospatial patterns from known settlements, teams combined statistical features with machine learning to surface subtle earthworks—circles, causeways, and low mounds—often invisible to the naked eye.

While Indigenous knowledge has long pointed to complex Amazonian landscapes, these predictions give field researchers a shortlist for reconnaissance, helping prioritize scarce time and resources for future expeditions.
